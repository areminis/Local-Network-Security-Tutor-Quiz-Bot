Lecture 25 – Security Policies, Governance & Risk Management (DETAILED + ELABORATED)

Overview:
Effective cybersecurity requires policies, oversight, and risk management beyond just technical controls.


Security Policies:
Formal documents defining acceptable use, security controls, and responsibilities.

Types:
• Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)
• Access Control Policy
• Incident Response Policy
• Data Protection Policy


Risk Management:
Identify → Analyze → Mitigate risk

Risk Formula:
Risk = Likelihood × Impact

Responses:
• Avoid
• Mitigate
• Transfer (Insurance)
• Accept


Compliance:
External requirements and regulations:
• HIPAA → Health data
• PCI-DSS → Payment card data
• GDPR → Privacy rules

Failure impacts:
• Fines
• Reputational damage


Incident Response:
Steps:
1) Preparation
2) Identification
3) Containment
4) Eradication
5) Recovery
6) Lessons learned

Security Frameworks:
• NIST CSF
• ISO 27001
Provide structured security practices.


Security Culture:
Employee training + awareness reduces phishing + insider threat
