Lecture 24 – Malware & Endpoint Security (DETAILED + ELABORATED)

Overview:
Malware is software designed to disrupt, damage, or gain unauthorized access to systems.


Types of Malware:
1) Virus – Infects files via replication
2) Worm – Self-replicating over networks
3) Trojan – Disguised as legitimate software
4) Ransomware – Encrypts files to demand ransom
5) Spyware – Collects sensitive data
6) Rootkits – Hide attacker presence

Attack Cycle:
1) Infection
2) Execution
3) Persistence
4) Command + Control

Delivery Methods:
• Email attachments
• Drive-by downloads
• USB / removable media
• Social engineering

Defense Techniques:
• Antivirus
• Endpoint monitoring
• Sandboxing
• Patch management
• Least privilege access

Ransomware Case Insight:
Attackers encrypt sensitive files, demand crypto payment.  
Defense:
• Backups
• Offline storage
• Email filtering
